Therapeutic Services

Families with young children come to us for a whole range of reasons.

Children

Behavioural and emotional problems, including:

  • anxiety
  • depression
  • disruptive behaviours
  • separation anxiety
  • trauma
  • grief and loss
  • adjustment disorders
  • social and emotional difficulties

Parents (mothers, fathers, foster and kinship carers, (e.g. grandparents), step parents

  • parenting stress
  • parenting difficulties related to
    • trauma (own or child’s)
    • grief and loss (own or child’s)
    • family separation or changes in care arrangements
    • own mental health problems e.g. anxiety, depression (including post natal anxiety and depression- either or both parents)
    • family crisis
    • difficult pregnancy or birth experience
    • family conflict or violence

We can provide individual and group sessions.As we are a team of three psychologists we can accommodate several members of one family in concurrent individual sessions.

Group programs may be run periodically when there is adequate demand.

The evidence based therapeutic approach we take with each client will depend on presenting difficulties, assessment and the goals we set with you.

We are qualified and experienced in using the individualised Circle of Security Intensive Interventionboth individually, with couples and groups. We also use Circle of Security Parenting DVD program as suited to client need. For more information about Circle of Security please refer to the official website atwww.circleofsecurity.net.

Other Services

Supervision

Clinical supervision for psychologists and other mental health professionals is available on request. Reflective supervision for infant mental health practitioners can also be provided.

For clinicians with requisite training in either intensive or parenting versions of the Circle of Security intervention, accredited supervision is available.

Assessment

Clinical assessment is a routine part of clinical practice. We also offer specialised assessments of family relationships and parenting, on a consultancy basis.

Training

Training can be provided in areas related to infant and early childhood mental health, early social and emotional development, family relationships and parenting.

Families in Mind Psychology also brokers training for practitioners and organisations in these areas.

Consultancy

Families in Mind psychologists can be contracted to provide specialised psychological services to clients and reflective supervision for practitioners, on behalf of your organisation.

Support is available to assist with program development and evaluation and professional development to enhance provision of services supporting families of young children. This may include specialist consultation to build capacity to foster infant and early childhood mental health and child social and emotional development.

Research

Families in Mind Psychology can collaborate to support research underpinning practice in infant and early childhood mental health services.

For any of these services please contact us directly to discuss your needs.
